## Two things worth leading with

**1. The brief's "What to build" was factually wrong, and I found it before writing code.** It named
four files and asserted the `## Output style` section "already exists in all four". Verified: the two
scaffold files have **no such section**, and the two root files are **regenerated from
`universal-rules.md` on every launch**. Building as written would have hand-edited two generated files
— silently overwritten at the next `fkit` launch — and two files with nothing to edit. Escalated; the
owner ruled the one-file version.

**2. The review found a launch-killer risk I had walked past.** The fkit-managed rules block has a
**4096B hard cap**, and exceeding it is `exit 1` inside init. The block was at **3438B — 84%** — and my
bullet had just consumed ~460B of the remaining headroom. **Nothing guarded it.** The source file
reads like ordinary prose; there is nothing at the edit site to tell an author a ceiling exists. The
next person to add a paragraph would have found out from a user with a dead launch.

## ⚠️ The caveat on "ready for done"

**This change is not behaviorally verified, and no test can verify it.** It is instruction text for
LLMs. The 3 new tests guard the *delivery mechanism* (size, markers, parity) — **not** whether agents
actually write more simply. Only owner observation over time shows that.

## Problems encountered

- **The brief was wrong** (above).
- **I claimed "no collateral changes" from init without auditing the gitignored surface**, and said so
  when asking for review. The reviewer audited it and found one: an earlier init refreshed `.claude/`
  while other tasks' sources were mid-edit, leaving `.claude/agents/fkit-coder.md` stale against
  `claude/agents/fkit-coder.md`. **Not a defect** — `.claude/` is gitignored and regenerated every
  launch — but my claim was incomplete, and the correction belongs on the record.

## Lessons learned

- **A hard limit with no guard at the edit site is a trap, not a limit.** `RULES_MAX` was documented in
  the script and invisible from `universal-rules.md`, which is where people actually type.
- **Naming what you did NOT verify gets it verified.** I told the reviewer I had not audited the
  gitignored surface; that is precisely where it found the one thing I had missed.
- **Preserve a split verdict, don't resolve it.** Codex said "no finding", the reviewer said medium.
  Recording the disagreement — and where each stood — is more useful than presenting a consensus that
  did not exist.
